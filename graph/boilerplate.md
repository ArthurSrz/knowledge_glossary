Standard reusable text reproduced verbatim across contracts, without negotiation. The term originates from 19th-century printing: metal plates used to reproduce standard text blocks were called boilerplates.

In M&A, boilerplate clauses include governing law, arbitration, and confidentiality — their wording is so consolidated by market practice that deviating from them signals a red flag rather than a legitimate ask. Contrast with [[bespoke]] (fully negotiated) and [[Model contract]] (template-derived but adaptable).
