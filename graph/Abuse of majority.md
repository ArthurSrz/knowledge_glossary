Abuse of majority (*abus de majorité*) is the case-law protection that limits what a controlling shareholder may impose on a minority — the counterpart to the minority's structural vulnerability. The Cour de cassation requires **two cumulative conditions**: a decision **contrary to the corporate interest** (*intérêt social*), taken **in the sole purpose of favouring the majority to the detriment of the minority**. The textbook case is the systematic transfer of profits to reserves combined with excessive remuneration paid to the majority, durably depriving the minority of any dividend. **The proof is notoriously difficult**: it is not enough that the majority receives advantages; one must establish a link between the decision and an unjustified or excessive advantage characterising a diversion at the minority's expense. A sole owner is subject to neither the [[constraint]] nor the protection. Distinct from, but symmetrical to, abuse of minority (a minority blocking a vital decision against the corporate interest).
