[Control (art. L.233-3 Code de commerce)](https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/codes/article_lc/LEGIARTI000031564650)

Control is the pivotal notion French company law uses to allocate corporate power — it reasons in terms of control, not of total ownership. Under article L.233-3 of the Code de commerce, one person controls another company notably when it holds, directly or indirectly, a fraction of the capital **conferring the majority of voting rights** in general meetings, when it **in fact determines the decisions** taken in those meetings through the votes it holds, or when it has the **power to appoint or remove the majority of the directors**. **Control is even presumed when a person holds more than 40% of the voting rights and no other shareholder holds more.** The consequence is that one can control a company without owning all of it (e.g. a 52.6% holder), and conversely a minority holder has no control regardless of the capital invested — the very gap that a [[Shareholders agreement]] is drafted to compensate by contract.
