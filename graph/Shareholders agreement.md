[Shareholders' agreement](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q3360276)

A shareholders' agreement (in French, *pacte d'associés*) is a contract between some or all the owners of a company that organises their rights and obligations beyond what the company's by-laws provide. It is not a statutory category of its own: French law does not name or regulate it as a specific contract, so it rests on **freedom of contract** (art. 1102 Code civil) and the **binding force of contracts** (art. 1193 Code civil). **Its defining trait is that it is *extra-statutory* — separate from the by-laws — which makes it confidential, binding only on its signatories, and generally sanctioned by damages rather than by nullity of the offending act**, unless the agreement itself organises specific enforcement. Practice has produced recurring families (investment / private-equity pact, founders' pact, family pact, joint-venture pact), but these are habitual combinations of clauses calibrated by context, **not legal types**: neither French law nor Wikidata recognises any sub-categories of the concept.
