[Blocking minority (art. L.225-96 Code de commerce)](https://www.legifrance.gouv.fr/codes/article_lc/LEGIARTI000042339458)

A blocking minority (*minorité de blocage*) is the stake that lets a shareholder prevent a decision without being able to impose one. It arises from the voting thresholds: in a *société anonyme*, the extraordinary general meeting — the only body able to amend the by-laws — **decides by a two-thirds majority of the votes cast** (art. L.225-96 Code de commerce). **Holding more than one third therefore blocks any statutory amendment**, while granting no power to carry anything through. Other thresholds give graded rights to even a small minority: from **5% of the capital**, a shareholder may put written questions to management or seek a court-ordered convening of a meeting (art. L.225-232, L.225-103). A holder below these thresholds (e.g. a 27% financial partner) has no legal blocking power at all — which is why such rights are recreated contractually as vetoes in a [[Shareholders agreement]]. A full owner has no use for any of this.
