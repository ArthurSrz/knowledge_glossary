The [[elicitation]] interview is an interview that aims for as fine-grained a description as possible of a past activity, carried out by a person in a professional practice [[situation]] or engaged in performing a task. Developed by psychologist and researcher [[Pierre Vermersch]] (CNRS, GREX), this set of techniques makes it possible to access dimensions of the lived experience of action that are not immediately present to the person's consciousness. The purpose of this interview is to gather information about both what actually happened and the implicit [[knowledge]] embedded in that action.
