What I have learned so far. Lawyers work at a [[contract]] level and demand [[clause]] [[precision]]. What I don't know is what an interview guide would look like for lawyers. How do you question [[lawyer]]s so they can more easily explain their work, what they do, and feel heard. [[Knowledge]] in my possession make me believe there could be first : 

1. Ask them questions about how they structure a legal document.
2. Ask them how their document is reviewed by another [[lawyer]] (more high level)
3. Dig into the [[boilerplate]] clauses that are on every kind of document and make it legally bounding (definitions, signatures, law, etc.)
4. Then a big set of questions on the models they use, and how much of it is to be adapted to special usecases. 
5. Document formatting is also key here : ask them to provide an example of documents and explain how they like it to be formatted (things that can later on be turned into [[design]] [[token]]s)
